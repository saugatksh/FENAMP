About Us

"Production and Market — The Foundation of Prosperity"

The Federation of National Production and Market Protection (FENAMP) has been established with a vision to build a self-reliant national economy rooted in Nepali originality by maximizing and mobilizing the country’s available resources. The federation aims to contribute significantly to the creation of an export-oriented and prosperous society through the promotion of production, industrialization, and market management in key sectors such as agriculture, education, health, and tourism.

In order to enhance the reach of Nepali products in both domestic and international markets, the federation is committed to ensuring quality production, collection, grading, packaging, labeling, and branding that meet global competitive standards. Furthermore, FENAMP is dedicated to the protection and promotion of local products, creating a conducive and efficient market environment for producers and entrepreneurs.

Bringing together organizations across the nation with shared objectives, the federation serves as an umbrella institution working to unite, empower, and advance Nepal’s productive sectors. The guiding motto — “Production and Market: The Foundation of Prosperity” — reflects the federation’s core belief that sustainable economic growth begins with strong domestic production and fair market systems.

About the Federation

FENAMP is a dynamic, inclusive, and forward-looking organization that connects farmers, entrepreneurs, producers, and traders from across Nepal under one platform. Recognizing that production and market development are the cornerstones of national prosperity, the federation promotes agricultural and industrial growth while ensuring efficient sale and distribution of goods.

Establishment

Officially established on Tuesday, 8th July 2025, the Federation was registered with the Kathmandu District Administration Office with participation from 21 district-level associations representing all seven provinces of Nepal.

This collective initiative marks the beginning of a national movement towards production-based economic independence and sustainable market development.
